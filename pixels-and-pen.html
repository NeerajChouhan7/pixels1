Who am I?
I’m Neeraj Chouhan, 21, in Mumbai. After high school I moved to Bangalore, started India’s first hacker house, The Residency. I helped founders raise funds, while I was building a content agency. Later, I moved to Mumbai, worked on India’s biggest commercials' sets for brands like Dove, Lakme, and Tata. I also produced and directed a commercial for Maya, we shot it across five cities, and now I’m building a company to nurture the next generation of filmmakers and stories from India to the world.
What do I believe in?
I believe quality storytelling outlasts algorithms and trends. Honest, well-crafted stories have lasting impact, and in a world where execution is increasingly automated and distribution is increasingly cheap, the durable advantage is the quality of the idea and the skill of the storytelling.
What am I building?
I’m building Pixels & Pen, a media company to discover, fund, and support the next generation of filmmakers. It combines a filmmaker program, a craft-focused media IP with masterclasses, podcast, and a newsletter. Our main focus will be the film festival, a theatrical screening event so that strong stories can be made, seen, and sustained.
